BUILD THE YOUTH AFRICA(BTYA) is a lead organization that was established in 2010 as a Community based organization but was officially registered in March/ 2013.

BTYA was founded by a team of two young men and no girls(Bwire Robert, Muyita Geofrey) in response to the increases catch of HIV/AIDS and other sexual related diseases in mafubira.

And the increasing levels of unemployment among the young people which at times would result into both increases dependence as well as the increases crime rates like commercial sex and robbery in Mafubira .

Key Activities

Evidence Generation BTYA does reviews in respect of a number of issues, for instance, establishing baseline of accountability mechanisms that exist in the division, determining the level of awareness of communities on Sexual Reproductive Health Rights and coverage of youth friendly services, etc. In addition, BTYA does evaluation studieis at quarterly and at the end of projects to strengthen project planning, implementation and development.

Main outputs and results

Strengthened capacity of both male & female youth in using local resources to make business and create jobs.

Provision of training in Sexual Health Reproduction

Provision of gender balanced technical skills

Improved access to resources for investment

Improved access to markets and market information

Develop channel which is comfortable for the young people enable them have access to youth friendly services

Problems or needs

HIV/AIDS: Uganda has been one of the countries most affected by the HIV epidemic, with a prevalence rate of 7.2 currently.

Early Motherhood: One in five (20%) of adolescent women 15-19 years covered in the 2011 UDHS had started reproductive life (16.3% were mothers already, and 3.4% were pregnant for the first time)

Access to SRH services: Limited access to SRHR services due to high illiteracy rate, long distances to health centres, cultural beliefs, and negative attitude on contraception, poor economic status, and unequal social status often affect their decision making on SRH matters.

BTYA aims at bringing about reduction in unemployment levels among young women and men in Jinja district

The primary approach is to build the capacity of young people to be able to take advantage of the opportunities available to them through acquisition of specialized knowledge and skills in using locally available resources and turning them into business opportunities

The more we increase the active participation and partnership with young people, the better we serve them. … And the more comprehensively we work with them as service partners, the more we increase our public value to the entire community.
